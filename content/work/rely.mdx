## The Brief

Rely — Real Estate Logistics for You — is a Bay Area startup founded in 2014, aiming to be the ultimate one-stop solution for renting and letting properties. The client provided raw interview data and asked us to transform it into a complete UX foundation: research synthesis, personas, user journeys, information architecture and design principles.

The platform targets a fragmented market. Renters juggle multiple listing sites. Landlords manage properties across disconnected tools. Neither side trusts the other enough to move quickly. Rely's ambition was to fix the entire pipeline.

## The Problem

Four core problems were identified from stakeholder interviews and market research:

**Prolonged Procedure** — The renting process is time-consuming and burdensome for both renters and landlords, causing frustration and delays at every stage.

**Obscure Information** — Lack of clarity around lease terms, additional costs and property details creates confusion on both sides of the transaction.

**Overwhelming Property Management** — Managing properties across disconnected tools creates stress for landlords, fragmenting their view of tenants, finances and maintenance.

**Engaging Across Multiple Platforms** — Renters spend hours across 5+ platforms to find the same listings, and still miss properties. Landlords duplicate work listing everywhere.

## Research Approach

The client conducted user interviews with renters and landlords. My job was to synthesise that raw data into structured insight and turn it into actionable design direction.

I compiled responses across **9 categories for renters** and **9 categories for landlords**, then ran stakeholder interviews to understand the business model, competitive landscape and product vision.

### What Renters Told Us

Renter insights clustered into: purpose of application, budget-centric features, communication and support, UI look and feel, security and privacy, customer care, accessibility and speed, personalisation, and platform performance.

The dominant pattern: renters wanted **speed and transparency**. They were tired of switching tabs, uploading the same documents multiple times, and waiting days for landlord responses on properties that were already rented.

### What Landlords Told Us

Landlord insights clustered into: purpose of application, tenant selection, communication and support, UI look and feel, document management, customer care, efficiency and automation, portfolio oversight, and reporting and analytics.

The dominant pattern: landlords wanted **trust and control**. Finding qualified tenants was their biggest anxiety. Document chaos was their biggest daily pain.

### Stakeholder Interview Key Findings

- Key partners are housing authorities, Section 8 landlords and landlord associations
- Business model: freemium with advertising and click-to-pay
- Web-first, mobile-responsive application
- Product should value ease of use, fewer touch points, trust with data, transparency and a fresh modern look
- "The competitors all look the same" — strong signal for differentiation opportunity
- Desired features: financial dashboard, document storage, tax integration, property finance management, networking

## The 5 Personas

The research surfaced 5 distinct user types spanning the rental ecosystem.

### Michelle — The Renter
*Full-time MBA student, 28, Female. Tech-savvy, extroverted, busy.*

Michelle is looking for a hassle-free, trendy experience finding and securing a rental. Her core needs: efficient property search by location, amenities and budget; finding roommates; transparency in lease terms and fees; seamless landlord communication; secure payments; control over her publicly visible data.

Her painpoints: time-consuming search across multiple platforms with different listings, unclear lease terms and additional costs, slow landlord response times, insecure transactions.

> *"Efficiency meets style — finding my perfect space with a click."*

### Brian — The Small Landlord
*Project Manager, 35, Male. Moderate tech comfort, patient, extremely busy.*

Brian owns 1 rental property as a side income source. He values tools that simplify process so he can focus on his primary job. His core needs: easy document repository for lease agreements and maintenance records; finding verified, qualified tenants; integrated communication; simplified property listing; financial tracking.

His painpoints: managing paper documents for each property, delayed communications due to no centralised channel, time lost listing on multiple platforms.

> *"Simplify, streamline, succeed — managing properties made easy."*

### Lizie — The Big Landlord
*Real Estate Investor, 55, Female. Tech novice, impatient, very busy, managing 16 properties.*

Lizie manages a substantial portfolio while also taking care of her children. She needs a streamlined one-stop solution. Her core needs: holistic tenant screening beyond just credit scores; a single dashboard for all properties; automated notifications across tenants; full portfolio overview.

Her painpoints: navigating multiple platforms for 16 properties, juggling family and property management, not being updated on property statuses, lack of a property manager forcing her to do everything herself.

> *"Scaling excellence — orchestrating success in property management."*

### Jake — The Housing Developer
*Developer looking for market intelligence and tenant pipeline tools.*

Jake needs data to make development decisions and wants a platform that connects him to renters and advocates early in the property lifecycle.

### Elisa — The Housing Advocate
*Advocate focused on fair housing, transparency and tenant rights.*

Elisa works to ensure the platform doesn't disadvantage vulnerable renters and wants visibility into how the platform handles data and decisions.

## Competitive Analysis

We analysed 5 competitors: **Zillow, TurboTenant, RentRedi, Avail, and Vero**.

Key insight from the analysis: all competitors focus on either renters or landlords. None successfully serve both sides with equal depth, and none address the broader ecosystem of developers and advocates.

Common gaps identified:
- Trust and transparency features are missing or surface-level
- Renting process is not genuinely simple or fast
- Limited rental filter options
- No social or community layer to help renters vet landlords and vice versa
- Competitors all look similar — no clear visual differentiation

**Rely's opportunity:** Be the first platform that truly serves all sides of the rental market with transparency, automation and a differentiated visual identity.

## Design Principles

Five principles guided all design decisions:

1. **Transparent by default** — Every fee, term and status is visible without needing to ask
2. **Reduce friction relentlessly** — Every unnecessary step is a step towards losing a user
3. **Trust is built in layers** — From verified badges to social connections to review history
4. **One place for everything** — No reason to leave the platform to complete a rental task
5. **Designed for both sides** — Every feature considers impact on renter AND landlord simultaneously

## Information Architecture

We built separate IA maps for three entry states:

**Pre-login:** Onboarding, property discovery, landlord profiles, and advocate resources — all accessible without an account to reduce friction.

**Renter IA:** Dashboard → Search & Filter → Property Detail → Application → Lease → Maintenance → Payments. An AI assistant (RileyAI) allows renters to ask natural language questions about their lease.

**Landlord IA:** Dashboard → Property Listings → Tenant Screening → Lease Creation → Maintenance Management → Financial Tracking → Analytics. Automated notifications and reminders reduce manual tracking.

## User Journeys

Four renter journeys were mapped: Explore Houses → Notification & Tour → Negotiation & Lease Viewing → Requesting Maintenance.

Four landlord journeys: Add Property Listing → Screen Tenants → Create Lease → Review Maintenance Requests → Track Financial Updates.

Each journey identified the specific steps, decision points and potential drop-off moments — giving engineering a clear picture of what to build and in what order.

## Outcome

This project delivered a complete UX foundation for Rely's v1 product:

- **5 detailed personas** covering the full rental ecosystem
- **Competitive analysis** across 5 platforms with opportunity mapping
- **9-category insight synthesis** from renter and landlord interviews
- **Stakeholder interview synthesis** informing product direction and business model
- **5 design principles** to guide all future design decisions
- **3 information architecture maps** (pre-login, renter, landlord)
- **8 user journey maps** across both primary user types
- **Concept diagram** showing how all user types interact with the platform

The research foundation positioned Rely to differentiate in a market where all competitors look the same — by designing for trust, transparency and a genuinely unified experience.
