## The Brief

Insurance distribution is a mess of disconnected systems. Agents, bank branches, sub-brokers and main brokers all needed to track proposals, policies, renewals, claims, commissions and customer data — but each was doing it across different tools, spreadsheets and phone calls.

Gemini Solutions was tasked with building a one-stop web platform for all distribution channel partners. I was the sole designer responsible for the entire experience — from research through to production handoff.

## The Problem

There was no unified digital platform for insurance distribution partners. Major gaps existed across all key functions:

- **No real-time visibility** — Agents had no live view of their proposals, policy statuses or renewal pipeline
- **Commission opacity** — Partners had to wait for paper statements to understand their earnings
- **Claims were manual** — Filing and tracking claims happened over email and phone
- **Knowledge was scattered** — Product information, training materials and circulars had no organised home
- **Hierarchy blind spots** — Main brokers couldn't see sub-broker performance without manual reporting

## Research

I led competitive analysis, technological research and design trend research across enterprise dashboard products globally. Key areas studied:

- How leading insurance platforms visualise complex multi-variable data
- Enterprise dashboard design patterns across financial services
- Data hierarchy and progressive disclosure in large-scale web applications
- How different partner types (agents vs brokers vs branches) differ in their information needs

The research established that data-heavy enterprise tools fail when they treat all users the same. Different partner types have fundamentally different mental models of what matters.

## The Solution

### Dashboard

The dashboard centralises an agent's entire business at a glance. After testing a data-heavy wireframe with multiple user groups of agents and partners, we simplified significantly based on feedback.

The final design organises information into distinct card groups:

- **Business standing cards** — Current agent tier and the key metrics affecting it
- **Policy and renewal cards** — Clean numerical display for selected time periods, no unnecessary graphs
- **Action items card** — Priority tasks requiring attention, surfaced automatically
- **Birthday card** — Relationship management tool allowing agents to send wishes via email or SMS directly from the dashboard

### Profiles — 4 Types, One System

The requirement was four distinct profiles: agent, bank branch, sub-broker and main broker. Rather than designing four separate experiences, I built a flexible profile system where data modules assemble differently based on hierarchy level.

Each profile surfaces the data most relevant to that partner type — agents see individual performance, main brokers see aggregate portfolio view with drill-down into sub-broker data.

### Policy Module

Policies involve enormous amounts of data. The design uses a two-level architecture:

**Table view** — All policies with filter by status (active, cancelled, dispatched) and product type. Search by policy holder name or policy number. One-click CSV export.

**Detail view** — Single policy expanded to show all data points, organised into clearly labelled sections by data category.

### Commissions Module

Commission data is displayed graphically to help agents immediately understand performance trends. Three sections:

1. Fortnightly commission graphs — visual trend at a glance
2. Commission statement download — one click to access or save
3. Recent proposals table — commission earned per recent transaction

### Claims Module

Claims are displayed as a date-sorted table with claim type, policy number and status. Each row expands into a full detail view. Two distinct action flows — file a claim and intimate a claim — were designed with separate layouts reflecting their different information requirements.

### Knowledge Bank

Modelled on a familiar file explorer pattern. Product icons differentiate folder, file, video and other content types. Universal search across the entire knowledge library. The familiarity of the pattern reduced the need for onboarding.

### Rewards & Recognition

Gamification layer to drive agent performance. Displays current points standing, projected achievements, and a timeline to the next reward tier. Competition banners and leaderboard visibility create motivation to perform.

## Outcome

The Partner Portal consolidated what was previously 3+ disconnected tools and manual processes into a single platform used by agents, bank branches, sub-brokers and main brokers across multiple distribution hierarchies.

Key design outcomes:
- **4 partner profile types** unified into one flexible template system
- **7 core modules** designed and shipped: dashboard, profiles, policies, commissions, claims, knowledge bank, rewards
- **Zero training required** on Knowledge Bank due to familiar file explorer pattern
- **Commission transparency** eliminated the wait for paper statements
- **Responsive design** extended the platform to mobile for on-the-go agents
